Sneak Peek of
The Seven Swords
Skylar Madison ran on the sidewalk, his feet thudding the ground and his backpack thwacking against his back. He barely looked ahead as he scrolled through the texts on his phone. Mostly his friends asking where he was. Roddy: Dude! Why’d U leave so soon? Carlo: We won the game. Missed you. Where’d you go? Skylar hitched his backpack over his shoulder as he finally glanced ahead. His house was three doors down. He could see the blue shutters from here. But a glance at the setting sun made his stomach turn. Late again. He’d lost track of time, once again, and was going to show up at home late for the third night in a row. Hopefully Mom would be forgiving. He slowed down as he neared the house, thumbs hovering over his phone as he tried to respond to the flurry of texts. He’d been at the rec center ten minutes before, and had tried his best to “disappear” and run home as soon as possible. School was long, and the rec was the only place that made the day feel worth it. He froze when he saw a text from Mom. Skylar, your sister has a fever again. She’s asking for you. Come home soon. Skylar looked at the time it was sent. Five o’clock. Shoot! That was nearly an hour and a half ago. Poor Lily. He really needed to keep track of time. He jogged up the cement walkway leading to his house, then took the steps two at a time. He fished a key out of his pocket, sticking it in the lock and turning it until it made a satisfying click. Skylar pushed the wooden door inward, the hinges letting off a small squeak. He stepped inside quietly, moving down the hall into the kitchen, where he glimpsed Mom sitting at the table, her head in her hands. “Mom?” She looked up abruptly. Her face shifted into something between anger and relief. “Hi, Skylar. Where have you been?” “I went to the rec and forgot to check my phone,” he said. “Do we have any food? And is Lily okay?” “She’s hot again. I got called to her school because she was feeling so muggy. And yes, there are a few slices of pizza in the fridge.” “Thanks, Mom.” He yanked open the fridge and pulled out the leftover pepperoni pizza, sliding it into the microwave. He glanced back at her. “Can I see Lily?” “She’s asleep now. But if you had come sooner, you would have been able to see her.” Skylar lowered his head. “Sorry, Mom.” “Skylar, you’re going to have to take a few weeks off from the rec center,” she said as she stood. “What? Why?” His head snapped up. No rec? Yeah, he’d stayed out a little too late, but this consequence? He needed the rec. She knew that. “You’ve been late three days in a row. I know you have friends, but you have family too. We need you.” That was when Skylar noticed the dark circles under her eyes and the worry lines above her eyebrows. She’d most likely been working from home all day. “Sorry, Mom,” he said again, and this time he meant it. “Thank you, son,” she said. Skylar decided not to mention his own problems with today. Mom sat back down at the table and flipped open her laptop. More work. She was always trying to get in as many hours as she could since… Skylar quickly popped open the microwave and pulled his plate of pizza out. He glanced at Mom, then ran up the stairs to his room. He yanked the door open then let it shut behind him, trying to shove down the emotions that threatened to spill over. He froze, his hand still on the doorknob. The pizza sagged dangerously over the edge of his plate. A soft, melodic sound drifted through the air. It sounded almost like a whistle, like a flute played by someone who actually knew what they were doing, not like the kids’ woodwind band at school who sounded like a bunch of dying birds. For the past few months, it had been like this. At random times, a song would slip into Skylar’s ears, with no source, no explanation. But it wasn’t just the music. Weird things had been happening. There was the book club. Skylar had been put into the book club at school. First of all, he hadn’t even learned to read until he was seven. And second of all, he hated books! Yet somehow, he’d been placed into a group with the nerdiest kids in school for a “fun” club. Then he had found out his two best friends—Carlo and Roddy, who were brothers—were moving. To Maryland. What was so good in Maryland that they didn’t have in Texas, Skylar didn’t know. Maybe good weather. And then these weird out-of-the-blue fevers that Lily kept getting. She would be pinned to the bed, suffocating by her own heat and too weak to move. She had to be pulled out of school for a week once, when she was so sick she couldn’t get up to use the bathroom. But thankfully she had gotten some sort of shot that had kept her good for awhile. But now it was back. The music faded. Skylar exhaled a breath he hadn’t realized he’d been holding, then sat on his bed. He crammed a mouthful of pizza into his mouth and chewed. Book club. Skylar definitely was not the sharpest tool in the box, but for some reason Skylar had been added to the team. At first he suspected Mom had something to do with it, but she’d been just as surprised as him. If only Dad were here. He was smart, an author actually, and would have been the perfect one to go to help to if he hadn’t… left. Something inside of Skylar twisted, and he blinked quickly to convince himself he was not crying. He accidentally bit the inside of his cheek. The sharp taste of blood mingled with his pepperoni pizza. He swallowed and shook his head, tossing his now empty plate into the garbage bag in his room. All of a sudden, he felt exhausted. He glanced at the digital clock sitting on his desk. Only seven. Too early to sleep. Skylar sprawled out on his bed and grabbed his homework. Pythagorean Theorem. Something to do with triangles. And letters. Gosh, how can they expect us to remember this all? It was only January, half way through the school year. Yet it was still warm outside. Texas weather made no sense. Skylar flipped through his textbook trying to find something that had to do with a theorem. An hour later, Skylar had moved on to his extra work he now had due to this “book club” he had apparently signed up for. Shoot! He’d forgotten to grab the book they were from his locker! Now he’d be even more behind. One more thing for them to laugh about. Maybe the new library would have it. Tomorrow was Saturday, the report wasn’t due until Monday. Tomorrow, he’d go to the library. Skylar leaned back on his bed, resting his arm across his forehead. The music came back. This time louder.
